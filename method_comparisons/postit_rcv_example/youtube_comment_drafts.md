# Commenting on the Post-it video — staying credible as a STAR proponent

Ready-to-post comment drafts for Equal Vote's ["Updated: How does RCV work? — With Post-its!"](https://youtu.be/Vte4nly_Neg), aimed at **RCV supporters who are open to new ideas**. Backed by the live elections [BV2176](bv2176_p8dp28_postit_rcv_example.md) / [BV2177](bv2177_v8r66y_seven_methods.md) / [BV2178](bv2178_8kg698_switch_made_real.md) and the [fair-and-balanced claim-check](postit_video_fair_and_balanced.md).

## The six rules the drafts follow

1. **Disclose your leaning in the first sentence.** "I lean STAR — check my math" beats being outed later. It converts advocacy into testimony.
2. **Verify before you praise.** Say the video's count *checks out* and cite the specifics (9–8, 3 exhausted, 10–9 head-to-head). Nothing builds standing with skeptics like having actually run the numbers.
3. **Concede your side's weak points unprompted.** The scores are the video's invention (score the same rankings stingily and STAR elects Purple too), and this electorate has *no* perfect winner — it's a cycle; even Blue loses to Green head-to-head. A comment that concedes something real is an argument; one that concedes nothing is a commercial.
4. **Meet RCV supporters on their own turf.** Their instinct — ranked ballots collect real information — is *correct*. The bridge is: keep the ballot, question the count. **Ranked Robin counts the exact same ranked ballots** by checking every head-to-head; no eliminations, no exhausted ballots. Offer that *before* STAR, then present STAR as the option that also reads strength of support. <!-- terminology-ok: public-facing guidance; "RCV supporters" is the audience's own label -->
5. **Invite clicking, not believing.** Link the live BetterVoting results — a neutral, interactive domain — as the primary reference; the GitHub lesson page is the deep-dive for the curious. (YouTube often shadow-hides comments with URLs: post the no-link draft first, then add links in a reply to your own comment, or write `bettervoting DOT com /v8r66y/results`.)
6. **Never call IRV's count wrong — call it incomplete.** The count is flawless; the complaint is what it never looks at. "It executed its rules perfectly, and its rules never check 10–9" lands; "RCV is broken" bounces.

## Which lesson to reference?

Lead with the **live election** (bettervoting.com/v8r66y/results) — it's interactive, neutral-looking, and makes the point in one screen: same ballots, four winners. Reference the **[fair-and-balanced claim-check](postit_video_fair_and_balanced.md)** (via the GitHub link) only for readers who want the full analysis — it's also your credibility receipt, since it stress-tests STAR's own case (the invented scores) as hard as IRV's.

What *not* to lead with: the Runoff Reversal, the RR tiebreak-ladder divergence, or STAR-PR — true, documented, and beside the point for a first conversation. One idea per comment: **the ballot is fine; the count never checks the matchup.**
