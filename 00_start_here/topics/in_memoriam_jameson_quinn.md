# In memoriam — Jameson Quinn (1974–2025)

*Jameson (James Arnold) Quinn, a statistician and voting-methods researcher whose work runs through this whole library, died on March 23, 2025, in a fall in Guatemala, where he lived with his wife. By several accounts he was trying to rescue a dog. He was 51.*

He earned a Ph.D. in Statistics from Harvard in 2020 — going back for the degree, by his own account, to sharpen his ability to say rigorously *how votes can be counted so that every voter has an equal voice*. That was the through-line of his work: bringing real statistical method to a field long argued by intuition and slogan.

## What he gave the field

- **Voter Satisfaction Efficiency (VSE).** Quinn popularized VSE — the modern way to compare voting methods by simulating thousands of electorates and scoring each method by how satisfied the average voter is with the winner it produces. It is now the field's standard empirical yardstick, and it is *load-bearing on the pages of this repo* (see [What makes a good winner?](what_makes_a_good_winner.md#measuring-it-empirically-vse--bayesian-regret), [strategic voting](strategic_voting.md), [election simulation models](election_simulation_models.md)). More than any single result, VSE changed *how the argument is had* — from "my method is fairer" to "here is the measured difference, under these assumptions."
- **The 3-2-1 voting method.** He designed [3-2-1](../../06_Other/three_two_one/README.md) (Good / OK / Bad, narrowed in three steps) — and this repo's clean-room 3-2-1 engine is verified against *his own* reference vectors, because no other authoritative implementation is cleanly licensed. His spec is why that page can exist.
- **E Pluribus Hugo.** He co-designed the **E Pluribus Hugo** nominating method adopted by the World Science Fiction Society for the Hugo Awards — a rare case of a voting theorist's design being put straight into real, contested use.
- **Peer-reviewed reform research.** With Sara Wolk and Marcus Ogren he co-authored *"STAR Voting, Equality of Voice, and Voter Satisfaction"* (Constitutional Political Economy, 2023) — bridging academic statistics and practical campaigning.

He served on the governing boards of **both** the Center for Election Science (Approval) and the Equal Vote Coalition (STAR). That he was an advocate takes nothing from the work; this library's habit of [disclosing affiliations](whos_who_voting_reform.md) is about reading claims critically, not discounting them — and Quinn's methods are cited here precisely because they hold up when you check them.
