# The benefits of STAR Voting

*One better ballot, and a whole list of election problems just… go away. You score each candidate 0–5 — like a five-star review — and the count does the rest. Here's the quick, exciting version of what that buys you.*

→ Want the **complete case, with every objection answered**? That's [Why STAR Voting](../../topics/Why_STAR_Voting.md). Just want the **mechanics**? [STAR — start here](../STAR_start_here.md).

<img src="../../img/star_ballot_example.png" width="460" alt="A real STAR ballot: five candidates (Andre, Blake, Carmen, David, Ella) each rated 0–5 stars, with the instructions — give your favorite(s) five stars, your last choice(s) zero; equal scores mean no preference; blanks count as zero; the two highest-scoring candidates are finalists and your full vote goes to the finalist you prefer">

*Rate each candidate 0–5 — equal scores allowed, blanks count as zero. That's the whole ballot. Everything below is what it gets you.*

---

## Vote your heart

Give the candidate you actually love a 5 **and** the front-runner you also like a 4 — both, at once. Scoring both high doesn't split your own side, so you never have to abandon a favorite or hold your nose for the "lesser evil." **Honesty is your best ballot.**

## No more spoilers

Because you rate every candidate on their own, a newcomer entering the race can't bleed support away from someone you already like. Vote-splitting — the reason a third candidate gets blamed for "spoiling" an election — stops happening, so new voices can run on their merits. → [the spoiler effect](../../topics/spoiler_effect.md)

## The winner really has majority support

STAR ends with an [automatic runoff](../the_count/STAR_Automatic_Runoff.md) between the top two, so whoever takes office actually beat the runner-up among voters who had a preference. **No more 30%-winners the other 70% opposed.**

## A ballot you already know

It's the five-star rating you use for movies and rideshares — rate them 0–5, done. Equal scores are fine, blanks are fine, and you can't accidentally spoil it, so error rates are low and nothing gets thrown away. → [filling out the ballot](../STAR_ballot_voting_styles.md)

## One election, not two

The runoff is built into the count — there's no separate second election. Where a place runs a primary-plus-general or two rounds today, STAR delivers the runoff **for free, in one election**: less cost, no second trip to the polls, and the *whole* electorate decides (second rounds usually draw far lower turnout).

## Anyone can check the count

STAR is [summable](../../topics/summability/): each precinct posts its score totals and a small head-to-head tally, and those numbers **add up — by hand — to the final result.** No shipping every ballot to one central computer. Transparent and auditable by design.

## Campaigns get nicer

To win, you need decent scores from your *opponents'* supporters too — so trashing a rival backfires when their voters can score you down. The rewarded move is outreach, not mudslinging, and the tendency is toward [broadly-liked winners](../../topics/what_makes_a_good_winner.md).

## One method for one seat or many

The same 0–5 ballot fills a single office, elects a slate ([Bloc STAR](../../../02_STAR_Bloc/)), or fills a whole body proportionally ([Proportional STAR](../../proportional_representation/STAR_PR/README.md)). One ballot everyone already knows, across the entire ticket.

## Try it in 30 seconds

Cast a STAR ballot right now at **[BetterVoting.com](https://bettervoting.com)** — then bring it to your next club, board, or HOA vote. Small real elections are how STAR spreads, and the [Equal Vote Coalition](https://www.equal.vote) is the place to get involved.

**And we mean all of it honestly.** STAR is a big improvement, not a magic wand: it *reduces* spoilers rather than eliminating them, it *usually* (not always) elects the head-to-head winner, and no method is un-gameable — that's a theorem, not a dodge. Every limit is laid out plainly in **[STAR's honest limits](../properties_and_limits/STAR_honest_limits.md)**, because a reform that's square about its edges is one you can trust on everything else.
