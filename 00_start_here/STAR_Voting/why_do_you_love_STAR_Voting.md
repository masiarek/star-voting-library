# "Adam, Why Do You Love STAR Voting So Much?"
### A recorded conversation — Larry (host) & Adam (expert)

A short, **personal** piece — the human "why," not the full technical case. Use it as a 2–3 minute **cold open** before the flagship episode, or as the **closer**. Where the flagship answers *"what's so good about STAR?"* analytically, this one answers *"why do YOU love it?"* — and that warmth is what makes people lean in.

## Segment 1 — the personal hook

**Larry:** Adam, you light up every single time STAR Voting comes up. So forget the charts for a second — why do you *love* it so much?

**Adam:** Honestly? Because it gave me back something voting had quietly taken away — the freedom to be honest. For most of my life, the ballot felt like a calculation: not "who do I actually want," but "who can win, so I don't waste my vote." STAR is the first ballot I've used where the honest move and the smart move are the same move. That's not a small thing. That's the whole game.

## Segment 2 — the three things I can't get over

**Larry:** Narrow it down. What actually moves you?

**Adam:** Three things, and they're not abstract for me.

**One — you never have to betray your favorite.** You can score the long-shot you love a 5 *and* the front-runner you can live with a 4. Nobody's vote splits, nobody gets spoiled. The first time I really understood that, it felt like a trick — and then I realized it's not a trick, it's just a better ballot.

**Two — every winner earns a real majority.** The automatic runoff means whoever takes office actually beat the runner-up, head-to-head, among the people who had a preference. No more winners with 35% of the vote that the other 65% actively opposed.

**Three — anyone can count it.** You add up the stars, then you add up the votes. A regular person can re-add the numbers at their kitchen table. After years of elections that felt like black boxes, that kind of transparency is almost emotional for me.

## Segment 3 — why I'm a fan but not a cheerleader

**Larry:** You're clearly a fan — but you're always careful. Why hold back?

**Adam:** Because I trust things that tell me their limits, and I want STAR to earn that same trust. It isn't magic — no voting method is, that's literally a theorem. STAR is strategy-*resistant*, not strategy-proof; it *usually* elects the candidate who'd beat everyone head-to-head, but not always. I love it *more* for being honest about that, not less. A reform that oversells itself is exactly how you get backlash — we've watched that happen. STAR earns trust by being square with you.

> [SLIDE] Full Deck — "What STAR Does (and Doesn't) Claim" and "Voting Theory Facts." [REPO] `00_start_here/topics/Why_STAR_Voting.md` — Slide 10.

## Segment 4 — the one-liner

**Larry:** Finish this sentence for me: "I love STAR Voting because…"

**Adam:** "…it lets me vote like I actually feel, it makes the winner truly earn it, and it lets me check the math myself. Honesty, majority, transparency — in one ballot you fill out in about ten seconds."

**Larry:** That's the trailer. Want to see it actually happen?

**Adam:** Let's run an election. *(→ hand off to the flagship episode.)*
