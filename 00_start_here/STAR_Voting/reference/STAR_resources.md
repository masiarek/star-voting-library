# STAR Voting — resources & further watching

*A curated set of good outside explainers, videos, and official guides for STAR Voting. Most are made by **advocates** (Equal Vote / STAR Voting and allied groups) — read them for the clear explanations, and keep this library's [honest-limits](../properties_and_limits/STAR_honest_limits.md) pages beside them for the caveats. For the runnable, example-first version of everything below, start with [STAR — start here](../STAR_start_here.md).*

## Watch (short → longer)

- **[STAR Voting Explained in Less than One Minute](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/Zy86WYmHvMk)** — a 60-second overview; the quickest possible "what is it."
- **[How Does STAR Voting Work?](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=fKg0fRL88zc)** — the standard short explainer of the two rounds (the video this repo's [start-here page](../STAR_start_here.md) cites).
- **[Equal Vote Coalition — intro talk](https://youtu.be/C_27pYcjsJs)** — a longer walk-through, including a proportional (multi-winner) example.

## Official guides & explainers

- **[Equal Vote Coalition — STAR](https://www.equal.vote/star)** — the canonical rules and the "equally weighted vote" framing (the source this repo defers to for ballot rules).
- **[STAR Voting Voter's Guide](https://www.starvoting.org/voters_guide)** — a **visual-first voter guide**: "5 = best, 0 = worst," an infographic of the count, and a myth-busting FAQ ("it's safe to vote your conscience," "leaving a candidate blank doesn't waste your vote"). Available in Spanish. A good model for explaining the *ballot* to first-time voters.
- **[starvoting.org](https://www.starvoting.org)** — the campaign hub (get-involved framing) · **[Proportional STAR](https://www.starvoting.org/star-pr)** — the multi-winner explainer.

## Real-world adoption (STAR in actual use)

**The current, authoritative "where is STAR used" list** is maintained by the campaign — [**starvoting.org/elections**](https://www.starvoting.org/elections) (we link it rather than copy it, because adoption status changes and a stale list is worse than none). The honest summary as of writing: used by organizations and political parties (Multnomah County Democrats since 2019, several Oregon state parties); not yet adopted for a government election. A concrete example:


- **[Multnomah County Democrats — "STAR Voting: What and Why"](https://multdems.org/star-voting-what-and-why/)** — a party organization explaining STAR (and **Bloc STAR** for multi-winner) *because they use it* for their own reorganization elections. Useful as a concrete "an organization actually adopted this, here's how they framed it to members" example, with a sample ballot.

## Deep dives & data

- **[Wolk, Quinn & Ogren (2023), "STAR Voting, equality of voice, and voter satisfaction"](https://doi.org/10.1007/s10602-022-09389-3)** — *Constitutional Political Economy* 34(3):310–334, **peer-reviewed and open access**. The primary source for both **VSE** and **PVSI** (the "backfire ratio" figures), and the strongest citation available for STAR's accuracy case. Free full text via [SharedIt](https://link.springer.com/epdf/10.1007/s10602-022-09389-3?sharing_token=0od88_U1nSyRqKjYdgfYUfe4RwlQNchNByi7wbcMAY5Flo8h-O2OXsGrN8ZvCJsAIKfmbq_BuMMDz1SCFtsHftLhH3jbjlacpdMgLufTvAkWOQP5bctzbgKm2vtDI3z846O5VnFLXamcNCgNI6y3Ys-oVd-DcxKbfs1xuMd6NAo%3D) · local copy [`wolk_quinn_ogren_2023_star_vse.pdf`](wolk_quinn_ogren_2023_star_vse.pdf). **Lean disclosed** (lead author directs the Equal Vote Coalition) and claim-checked here: **[the foundational STAR paper, claim-checked](wolk_quinn_ogren_2023.md)**.
- **[Voter Satisfaction Efficiency (VSE)](https://electionscience.github.io/vse-sim/)** — the simulation metric ([Jameson Quinn](../../topics/in_memoriam_jameson_quinn.md)'s work) behind "STAR scores well on satisfaction"; the interactive chart itself is [vse-graph.html](https://electionscience.github.io/vse-sim/vse-graph.html) (strategy view: [stratstuff.html](https://electionscience.github.io/vse-sim/stratstuff.html)). Read with [election simulation models](../../topics/election_simulation_models.md) on why every such number is model-conditional.
- **[Marcus Ogren — Voting in the Abstract](https://voting-in-the-abstract.medium.com/)** — field-wide analysis from a *Condorcet-leaning* researcher; a useful outside-the-STAR-bubble perspective.
- **[electowiki: STAR voting](https://electowiki.org/wiki/STAR_voting)** · **[Wikipedia: STAR voting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/STAR_voting)** — encyclopedia references.
- **[Divided We Fall — "Can STAR and Approval Voting Fix Our Elections?"](https://dividedwefall.org/star-and-approval-voting/)** (2023) — a rare **joint STAR + Approval case** in one essay, on a civil-debate outlet. Lean disclosed: the authors lead STAR Voting Action and Utah Approves — advocates, writing well. Its best hard datum is the 2021 St. Louis Approval primary (winner ~35% under choose-one framing vs 57% approval) — verify against a primary source before citing it as fact here.
