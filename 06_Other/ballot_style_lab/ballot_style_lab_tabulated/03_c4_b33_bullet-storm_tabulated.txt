election_title: Bullet Storm — a bullet-voting electorate and the few who spread

scenario_description: |-
  Three brigades bullet-vote their champion (5 and silence - many literally
  leave the rest blank), while a thoughtful minority spreads honest scores.
  The lesson lives in the Automatic Runoff: a bullet ballot whose champion
  missed the top-two scored both finalists 0 - it goes EQUAL SUPPORT, silent
  by its own choice, and the runoff is decided by the voters who gave the
  count something to work with. Bullet voting is legal, counted - and
  self-muting.

  The electorate (33 ballots, all individual rows):
    - Astrid brigade: 12 voters (8 bullet, 4 strong_backup)
    - Boris brigade: 7 voters (6 bullet, 1 weak_backup)
    - Carla brigade: 8 voters (8 bullet)
    - the thoughtful few: 6 voters (5 nuanced, 1 ranked_style)

  Ballot styles in play (taxonomy: 00_start_here/STAR_Voting/
  STAR_ballot_voting_styles.md):
    - weak_backup: 5 + a grudging 1 backup, rest 0/blank
    - strong_backup: 5 + a strong 4 backup, rest 0/blank
    - bullet: choose-one transplant: favorite 5, rest 0/blank
    - nuanced: full 0-5 range, honest gaps, ties allowed
    - ranked_style: uses each score once, like a ranking

  What the frozen seed produced: Scoring Round Carla 73, Astrid 67, Boris 39,
  Dolores 27. Runoff Astrid 13 vs Carla 13 (Equal Support 7). The runoff
  itself is TIED - broken by the next official tiebreaker (higher scoring
  total). STAR winner: Carla. Choose-One (Plurality) would elect Astrid.
  RCV-IRV would elect Astrid. The engine flags the classic center-squeeze
  signature (RCV-IRV the lone outlier).
