  The center-squeeze profile - two big poles who each rate consensus-Ben an
  honest second - but built from NOISY utilities and mixed ballot styles
  instead of clean textbook blocs. The question the textbook can't answer:
  does the squeeze still happen once real-world mess is layered on? Tabulate
  and see whether RCV-IRV's first-choice tunnel vision eliminates the
  candidate the pairwise count says beats everyone.

  The electorate (38 ballots, all individual rows):
    - Ava pole: 13 voters (6 ranked_style, 3 strong_backup, 3 nuanced, 1 harsh)
    - Cora pole: 20 voters (11 weak_backup, 4 ranked_style, 3 nuanced, 2 slate)
    - Ben middle: 5 voters (4 nuanced, 1 gentle)

  Ballot styles in play (taxonomy: 00_start_here/STAR_Voting/
  STAR_ballot_voting_styles.md):
    - gentle: gentle soul: everything squeezed into 3-5, no zeros
    - nuanced: full 0-5 range, honest gaps, ties allowed
    - strong_backup: 5 + a strong 4 backup, rest 0/blank
    - weak_backup: 5 + a grudging 1 backup, rest 0/blank
    - ranked_style: uses each score once, like a ranking
    - slate: partisan slate: equal 5s for the in-group, 0 outside
    - harsh: harsh grader: everything squeezed into 0-2

  What the frozen seed produced: Scoring Round Cora 124, Ben 111, Ava 80.
  Runoff Ben 19 vs Cora 18 (Equal Support 1). STAR winner: Ben. Condorcet
  winner Ben (agrees). Choose-One (Plurality) would elect Cora. RCV-IRV would
  elect Cora. Approval would elect Cora. The engine flags the classic
  center-squeeze signature (RCV-IRV the lone outlier).
